SESSION 1-1

Introduction to Programming

Speaker: Dr. John Smith Date: July 15, 2011 Time: 09:00 - 10:30 Room: Conference Hall A
Print

Abstract

This introductory session provides students with fundamental programming concepts and problem-solving techniques. Through hands-on exercises and interactive demonstrations, participants will learn the basics of programming logic, variables, control structures, and functions.

The session is designed for beginners with little to no programming experience and aims to build a strong foundation for further study in computer science. Students will work with simple algorithms and learn how to think computationally.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand Programming Basics: Learn fundamental concepts of programming including variables, data types, and operators
  • Control Structures: Master conditional statements and loops for program flow control
  • Problem-Solving Skills: Develop algorithmic thinking and decomposition techniques
  • Practical Application: Write and test simple programs to solve real-world problems

Speaker Biography

Dr. John Smith is an Associate Professor of Computer Science at National Tsing Hua University. With over 10 years of teaching experience, he specializes in programming education and has developed innovative curricula for introducing students to computer science concepts.
