SESSION 3-2

Advanced Topics in Computer Science Education

Speaker: Dr. Jane Smith Date: July 15, 2011 Time: 14:30 - 16:00 Room: Conference Hall B
Print

Abstract

This session explores cutting-edge approaches to computer science education at the high school level. We will discuss innovative teaching methodologies, curriculum development strategies, and the integration of modern programming languages and tools into the classroom environment.

Participants will learn about practical approaches to engage students in computational thinking, problem-solving, and collaborative software development. The session includes case studies from successful programs and interactive discussions on overcoming common challenges in CS education.

Key Topics

  • Computational Thinking: Developing problem-solving skills through algorithmic approaches
  • Modern Programming Languages: Introduction to Python, JavaScript, and visual programming tools
  • Project-Based Learning: Engaging students through real-world applications
  • Collaborative Development: Teaching version control and teamwork skills
  • Assessment Strategies: Effective methods for evaluating student progress

Speaker Biography

Dr. Jane Smith is a Professor of Computer Science Education at National Tsing Hua University. With over 15 years of experience in curriculum development and teacher training, she has been instrumental in shaping computer science programs across Taiwan and internationally.

Her research focuses on pedagogical approaches for teaching programming concepts to high school students and the development of educational tools that make computer science accessible to all learners.

About HSCC

The High School Computer Conference brings together educators, students, and industry professionals to advance computer science education.
